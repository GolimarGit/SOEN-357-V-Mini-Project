User Research and Persona Creation

User Research

The first step for designing a user interface (UI) for any software is user research. The data gathered will be used to identify user goals and frustrations related to software usage. For conveniency, the method for collecting data will be through a survey. Participants will be handed slips of paper with questions related to their experience with doctor visits via booking appointments through online booking systems or via walk-in. To make sure that answers are representative of the targeted demographic, the survey was handed out at 2 health clinics that both offer walk-in and by-booking appointments.

The survey is designed to guide the user through questions that follow a logical flow: demographic questions, their current appointment process, their willingness to use a mobile app, and desired features for a healthcare app. Starting from general questions to user suggestions on the applications will make the user feel less pressured when answering questions. To maintain anonymity, no sensitive information pertaining to their identity (name, phone number, etc.) was included in the survey.

Personas

The personas were created based on the responses collected in the survey, ensuring they reflect the needs, preferences, and concerns of potential users of the health app. The data shows that phone calls are the most common booking method, long wait times and scheduling difficulties are major pain points, and many users value friendly staff and clear communication. Additionally, a significant number of respondents prefer manually selecting their doctor, and privacy concerns are a common barrier to app adoption. These insights shaped the personas by aligning their goals, motivations, and challenges with real user responses, ensuring the app design caters to the actual needs of its intended audience.


Emily Thompson—The Busy Professional

Age: 27

Occupation: Software Project Manager

Healthcare Needs: Occasional clinic visits for checkups and minor issues

Pain Points:

  • Long wait times for scheduling
  • Difficulty finding available slots outside of work hours
  • Prefers not to wait on hold when calling clinics

Motivations:

  • Wants a fast and easy way to book appointments
  • Prefers real-time availability to plan efficiently
  • Values automated reminders so she doesn't forget appointments

How She Uses the App:

  • Books appointments via the app instead of calling
  • Uses reminders to manage her schedule
  • Checks wait times to avoid long delays

Robert Martinez—The Frequent Patient

Age: 52

Occupation: Accountant

Healthcare Needs: Regular visits due to a chronic condition

Pain Points:

  • Prefers calling but often experiences long wait times
  • Needs to see the same doctor regularly
  • Finds keeping track of past appointments difficult

Motivations:

  • Wants a simple way to book follow-up visits
  • Prefers phone support options but is open to an app
  • Wants to track appointment history easily

How He Uses the App:

  • Manages follow-ups with his regular doctor
  • Uses appointment history to track past visits
  • Receives reminders for upcoming appointments

Margaret Collins—The Caregiver

Age: 63

Occupation: Retired, cares for husband and grandchildren

Healthcare Needs: Schedules appointments for both her and family members

Pain Points:

  • Navigating complex clinic systems
  • Forgets appointments without reminders
  • Prefers human interaction but dislikes long phone wait times

Motivations:

  • Needs family scheduling to manage multiple appointments
  • Wants reminders for medications and doctor visits
  • Prefers a simple, easy-to-read app interface

How She Uses the App:

  • Books appointments for herself and her husband
  • Uses notifications to remind her of upcoming visits
  • Checks telehealth options for virtual consultations
